## 3. Scientific Insights

### 1. The Necessity of Sequential Calibration (EM)
The **w/o EM (Kalman)** variant achieves near-zero trajectory recovery (**0.00%**). This proves that traditional signal processing filters are incapable of handling the systematic 45px vertical drift that shifts gaze to the wrong text line. Our **STOCK-T** iterative calibration is mandatory for webcam-scale hardware failure.

### 2. POM as the Robustness Engine
The **STOCK-T (Edge)** variant, which utilizes our Psycholinguistic-Oculomotor Model (POM) priors, maintains an overall trajectory recovery of **58.88%**. When POM is removed (**w/o POM**), recovery drops significantly (from 58.88% to 24.47%), confirming that biological reading priors provide the critical "structural gravity" needed to break line-locking traps.

### 3. Surprisal Sensitivity (The "Noise Trap")
The **Edge (Uniform)** variant (13.86% Acc) slightly outperforms the **Surprisal** variant (12.33% Acc). 
- **Interpretation**: While high-level linguistic features (BERT Surprisal) are theoretically superior, they can introduce local jitter in high-noise environments. The structural stability of the POM oculomotor prior is more reliable for strict word identification under extreme drift.

### 4. Cross-Linguistic Stability (L1 vs L2)
- **Trajectory Recovery**: L1 and L2 populations show remarkably similar recovery rates (Difference: 3.66%), proving STOCK-T is a universal gaze decoder.
- **Precision**: L1 readers show slightly higher word accuracy, likely due to more rhythmic and predictable saccade patterns compared to bilingual readers.
