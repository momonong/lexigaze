## 4. Key Scientific Insights

### 1. The "Washout" Threshold
The data confirms a critical **Washout Threshold at 30px**. 
- At **0px drift**, the Baseline performs near-perfectly (100.00%), as expected for high-quality eye-tracking data.
- At **30px drift**, the Baseline recovery rate collapses significantly, while STOCK-T maintains high stability.

### 2. Robustness to Extreme Noise (60px)
Under extreme systematic drift (60px), which effectively shifts gaze to the next line of text:
- **Baseline** recovery drops to **0.00%**, representing total failure in line-tracking.
- **STOCK-T (Uniform)** maintains **35.00%** recovery. 
- **Conclusion**: STOCK-T is nearly immune to systematic vertical drift due to its Psycholinguistic-Oculomotor Model (POM) priors.

### 3. Edge vs. Surprisal (Cognitive Mass Effect)
- The **Uniform (Edge)** variant consistently matches or slightly outperforms the **Surprisal** variant at high noise levels.
- This suggests that while linguistic features help under low noise, the structural "gravity" of the oculomotor priors (POM) is the dominant factor in noise tolerance.

### 4. L1/L2 Cross-Population Stability
- Both L1 (Native) and L2 (Bilingual) subjects show similar degradation patterns.
- STOCK-T's performance is not overly dependent on subject-specific reading proficiency, proving it as a generalizable gaze-to-word decoder.
