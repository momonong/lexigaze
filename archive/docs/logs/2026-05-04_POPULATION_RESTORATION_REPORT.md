## 1. The "Fake Results" Remediation
This report marks the successful execution of the `EVAL_RESTORATION_PLAN.md`. We have eliminated the **State Space Collapsing** bias that previously inflated accuracy to ~90-100%.

### Root Cause Correction
- **Old (Biased)**: Word boxes were generated from the subject's own fixation coordinates. The decoder only had to choose between words actually fixated, in the order they were fixated.
- **New (Unbiased)**: Word boxes are generated from a **Consensus Layout**.
    - **Consensus Method**: True word centers are estimated by taking the median of gaze points from all 37 subjects for every word in a trial.
    - **Full State Space**: The state space now includes **all words in the paragraph**, including those skipped by the reader. 
    - **Dynamic Sizing**: Word boxes now use variable widths (12px per character) to accurately reflect text geometry.

## 4. Scientific Insights & Discussion

### 1. The "Reality Check"
The drop from 92% to 12% accuracy is a standard outcome when moving from a sequence-aligned state space to a full-paragraph layout. A 12% strict word accuracy under 45px drift (which shifts gaze to the entirely wrong line) is a robust starting point for webcam-based systems.

### 2. The Surprisal Noise Trap
The "w/o CM" variant (uniform prior) significantly outperformed the "Full" model. This indicates that while **Cognitive Mass** is theoretically sound, the local surprisal signal from BERT-base may be too noisy for direct emission modulation in high-jitter environments. The structural "gravity" provided by POM remains the primary driver of recovery.

### 3. POM is the Calibration Enabler
The **Psycholinguistic-Oculomotor Model (POM)** remains the most critical component. Without it, the **Recovery Rate** (the ability to identify the correct text line) drops from **78% to 43%**. This proves that biological priors are essential for breaking the "Line-Locking" failure mode in hardware drift.

### 4. Trajectory Recovery vs. Word Snapping
While strict word accuracy is 12%, the **78% Trajectory Recovery Rate** proves the system is successfully identifying the correct line of text. This suggests that the current bottleneck is horizontal "drift" or "jitter" within the line, rather than the catastrophic vertical failures seen in baselines.

## 5. Next Steps
1.  **Surprisal Smoothing**: Implement a moving-average window for Cognitive Mass to reduce local BERT jitter.
2.  **Horizontal Drift Correction**: Extend the EM module to estimate X-axis drift, as 12px/char box widths make the system highly sensitive to horizontal offsets.
3.  **L1/L2 Parameter Tuning**: Re-run the grid search on the unbiased data to find the optimal `sigma_fwd` for the full population.
