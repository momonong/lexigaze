Title: The STOCK-T Architecture (Spatio-Temporal Oculomotor-Cognitive Kalman Transformer)

A comprehensive Neuro-Symbolic architecture for robust eye-tracking on edge devices.

1. Input Layer: A dual-input stream consisting of a Noisy Webcam Gaze Stream (time-series of x,y coordinates with jitter and drift) and the Target Text Context.

2. Linguistic Feature Extraction (BERT): A transformer-based module within STOCK-T that processes the Target Text to compute word-level features. It outputs 'Surprisal' (via Masked Language Modeling) and 'Attention Centrality' (via mean self-attention weights). These are multiplied to produce 'Cognitive Mass' (CM) for each word.

3. Psycholinguistic-Oculomotor Model (POM): The STOCK-T transition matrix generator that receives CM values. It models three causal components: 
    - Forward Momentum: Gaussian saccadic priors toward word (i+1).
    - Skipping Penalty: Forward transitions are modulated by the CM of target words.
    - Regression Boost: Backward transitions (i -> j, where j < i) are boosted by the CM of the current word.

4. Neural Perception Likelihood: A module in the STOCK-T pipeline that maps the Gaze Stream to word bounding boxes. It uses a 2D Gaussian likelihood model P(G|W) and incorporates 'Optimal Viewing Position' (OVP) offsets, shifting the word centers to 35% of their width to align with biological foveation.

5. Multi-Hypothesis EM Initialization: The STOCK-T diagnostic probing block that evaluates three vertical drift hypotheses (H = [0, +LineHeight, -LineHeight]). It runs a preliminary Viterbi pass for each hypothesis and selects the one with the maximum global likelihood to initialize the fine-grained drift correction.

6. Spatio-Temporal Viterbi Decoder: The STOCK-T core fusion engine that performs sequence decoding over the hidden states (words). It integrates the POM Transition Matrix and the Neural Perception Likelihoods to find the optimal global reading path.

7. Output: The system outputs a 'Decoded Semantic Path' (the sequence of words the user actually read) and 'Calibrated Gaze Coordinates' where the systematic drift has been removed.
