## 2. Methodology Update

### A. Optimal Viewing Position (Skill 7)
Biological eye-tracking research shows that foveation is not centered on a word but shifted towards the **OVP** (approx. 35% of word width).
- **Implementation**: Adjusted $\mu_x$ for emission probabilities.
- **Adaptive Variance**: Integrated width-dependent $\sigma_x$ to allow more horizontal tolerance for longer words.

### B. EM-Viterbi Auto-Calibration (Skill 6)
To handle unknown hardware offsets (systematic drift), we implemented a two-pass "Expectation-Maximization" loop:
1.  **E-Step**: Use the base Viterbi decoder on the first 30 frames to predict intended word targets.
2.  **M-Step**: Calculate the **Median Error** between noisy gaze points and predicted word centers.
3.  **Correction**: Shift the entire trajectory by the estimated drift vector and re-decode.

## 4. Discussion

### The Failure of Traditional Smoothing
The **Kalman Filter** performed exceptionally poorly (2.6%). This highlights a critical insight for the paper: traditional signal processing filters high-frequency noise but "locks in" systematic drift. Without the **Neuro-Symbolic** "knowledge" of the text, temporal smoothing actually makes it harder to recover the true reading path.

### Reaching 50%
The **EM-AutoCal** reached 50.0% accuracy. This is a psychologically significant threshold for usability in reading assistants. Even with a massive +45px vertical error (half a line height), the system correctly identifies 1 out of every 2 words by observing the user's reading rhythm and linguistic context.

## 5. Conclusion
We have moved from a simple point-based "snap" (16.7%) to a self-correcting temporal sequence decoder (50.0%). The next phase of research should focus on **Dynamic Drift Tracking**, updating the drift vector continuously throughout the trial to account for head movement.
