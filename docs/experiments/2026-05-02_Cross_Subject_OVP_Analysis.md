## 4. Scientific Discussion: The "Robust Center" Insight
For the NeurIPS paper, we must reconsider the role of OVP in edge eye-tracking:
1.  **Noise Masking**: Under extreme +45px drift and $\sigma=40px$ jitter, the fine-grained biological advantage of foveating the OVP (shifted by only ~10-15px for average words) may be "masked" by the hardware error.
2.  **Screen Reading Behavior**: Human foveation patterns on high-brightness laptop screens might differ from paper-based reading (the source of OVP theory), with users potentially defaulting to word centers for better contrast or due to UI-induced cognitive load.
3.  **L2 Deliberation**: The original "OVP Anomaly" insight remains valid—L2 readers definitely target word centers more deliberately. However, this study shows that native readers *also* perform excellently (or even better) with center-snapping under high-noise conditions.
