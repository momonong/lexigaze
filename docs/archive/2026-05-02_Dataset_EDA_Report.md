# Experiment Report: L1 vs. L2 Dataset Descriptive Statistics (EDA)

**Date**: 2026-05-02  
**Project**: LexiGaze (Neuro-Symbolic Gaze Calibration)  
**Status**: Statistical Foundation Established

## 1. Objective
This report documents the implementation of **Skill 20**, providing a quantitative foundation for the behavioral differences between Native (L1) and Bilingual (L2) readers in the GECO dataset. These statistics justify the design of the **Psycholinguistic-Oculomotor Model (POM)** used in our final STOCK-T architecture.

## 2. Methodology
- **Dataset**: Ghent Eye-Tracking Corpus (GECO).
- **L1 Group**: Native English speakers (Subjects: `pp01`, `pp03`, `pp05`, `pp07`, `pp09`).
- **L2 Group**: Dutch-English Bilinguals reading English (Subjects: `pp01`, `pp02`, `pp03`, `pp04`, `pp05`).
- **Sample Size**: 10 trials per subject (Total 100 trials).
- **Clean Data**: Analyzed original coordinates before noise injection.

## 3. Results Summary

| Metric | L1 (Native) Mean | L2 (Bilingual) Mean | Difference |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Fixation Duration** | 287.8 ms | 354.3 ms | +23.1% (L2 is slower) |
| **Skipping Rate** | 44.1% | 41.5% | -2.6% (L1 skips more) |
| **Regression Rate** | 30.7% | 32.5% | +1.8% (L2 regresses more) |
| **Saccade Amplitude** | 5.7 words | 4.2 words | -26.3% (L2 has smaller jumps) |

## 4. Psycholinguistic Justification for STOCK-T
The observed differences support our Neuro-Symbolic approach:
1. **L2 Cognitive Penalty**: The significantly higher fixation duration in L2 readers justifies our use of **Cognitive Mass (CM)** to modulate emission probabilities.
2. **Saccadic Momentum**: L1 readers take much larger forward jumps (5.7 words), supporting a strong saccadic momentum prior in native reading models.
3. **Regression Dynamics**: L2 readers exhibit higher regression rates and shorter saccades, necessitating the `gamma` and `sigma_reg` parameters in our **POM Transition Matrix** to capture non-linear look-backs during difficult word processing.
